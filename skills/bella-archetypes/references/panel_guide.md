# BellaAssist Archetype Panel Guide

## Panels

| Panel | Archetypes | When to use |
|-------|------------|-------------|
| **SC Panel** | SC-01 through SC-04 | B2B positioning, enterprise messaging, pricing models, SC-facing features, go-to-market strategy |
| **Participant Panel** | P-01 through P-04 | Consumer messaging, accessibility evaluation, onboarding UX, participant-facing features, Easy Read content |
| **Full Panel** | All 8 | Comprehensive brand evaluation, cross-market alignment, launch readiness |

## Strategic Pairs

For focused 2-voice debates, use these pre-defined pairings. Each tests a specific tension in the product/brand.

| Pair name | Archetypes | Tension tested |
|-----------|------------|----------------|
| **trust-vs-efficiency** | SC-02 (Evidence-Driven Advocate) + SC-01 (Growth Pragmatist) | Whether messaging balances evidence protection with ROI. SC-02 asks "Can I trust this?" while SC-01 asks "What's the payoff?" |
| **power-vs-simplicity** | P-01 (Empowered Navigator) + P-02 (Supported Participant) | Whether concept works for power users AND overwhelmed users. P-01 rejects oversimplification; P-02 rejects jargon. If both approve, the concept threads the needle. |
| **fighter-vs-maintainer** | P-03 (Proactive Parent-Advocate) + P-04 (Anchoring Carer) | Whether concept serves advocates AND stable-state carers. P-03 wants ammunition; P-04 wants burden reduction. |
| **solo-vs-enterprise** | SC-01 (Growth Pragmatist) + SC-03 (Institutional Navigator) | Whether positioning works across business structures. SC-01 evaluates as a sole trader; SC-03 evaluates for team procurement. |
| **craft-vs-conviction** | SC-04 (Discerning Craftsperson) + SC-02 (Evidence-Driven Advocate) | Whether the quality-check and evidence-protection lenses align. SC-04 asks "Is this better than what I built?" SC-02 asks "Will this protect my participants?" |
| **cross-advocacy** | SC-02 (Evidence-Driven Advocate) + P-03 (Proactive Parent-Advocate) | Both advocacy-driven from different sides — professional SC vs. family member. Do they see the same value in the product? |
| **cross-high-agency** | SC-01 (Growth Pragmatist) + P-01 (Empowered Navigator) | Both self-directed and tech-capable. SC-01 is the buyer; P-01 is the end user. Does the product work for both? |

## Divergence Signal Framework

After collecting all voices, interpret the pattern:

### Cross-panel divergence
- **SC agrees, P disagrees** - Messaging is too B2B. Accessible enough for professionals but not for participants or carers. Consider: simplify language, add Easy Read layer, lead with human benefit not efficiency.
- **P agrees, SC disagrees** - Messaging is too consumer-facing. Participants feel seen but SCs don't see the business case. Consider: add ROI metrics, evidence-quality positioning, professional credibility signals.
- **Both agree** - Strong signal of broad appeal. But verify it's not blandness — check whether each archetype's specific hot-button was actually tested.
- **Both disagree** - Concept needs fundamental rework. Re-examine core value proposition.

### Within-SC divergence
- **SC-01 + SC-04 agree, SC-02 + SC-03 disagree** - Efficiency-focused concept that fails the trust/compliance test. The pragmatists like it; the protectors don't.
- **SC-02 + SC-03 agree, SC-01 + SC-04 disagree** - Evidence/compliance concept that feels too heavy for practitioners. The institutional voices approve but solo operators see overhead.
- **SC-03 alone disagrees** - Concept doesn't translate to enterprise/organisational contexts. May need team dashboard, audit trail, or procurement pathway.
- **SC-02 alone disagrees** - Trust or privacy concern. This archetype is the hardest to win and the most important for credibility. Investigate the specific objection.

### Within-Participant divergence
- **P-01 + P-03 agree, P-02 + P-04 disagree** - Power-user concept that fails accessibility. High-agency users love it; those who need support find it overwhelming or irrelevant.
- **P-02 + P-04 agree, P-01 + P-03 disagree** - Simplified concept that feels patronising to capable users. May need tiered messaging or power-user features.
- **P-03 alone disagrees** - Messaging doesn't acknowledge the fight. Parent-advocates are system-aware and reject naive optimism.
- **P-04 in crisis mode disagrees** - The concept adds burden. This is the hardest test — if it works for a carer in crisis, it works for everyone.
