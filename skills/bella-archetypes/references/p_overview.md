# Participant Archetype Overview

Data foundation: 14 co-design sessions, 10 individuals across 8 interview sets, ~134K words.

Critical structural decision: **self-voicing participants and carers are modelled as separate archetypes**, supported by proxy-reporting research.

## Comparison Table

| Dimension | P-01: Empowered Navigator | P-02: Supported Participant | P-03: Proactive Parent-Advocate | P-04: Anchoring Carer |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Voice** | Self-voicing adult | Self-voicing adult | Carer/parent | Carer/parent |
| **Agency** | Very high | Moderate (supported) | Very high | Variable (crisis to stable) |
| **Individuals** | Kevin | Kylie, Melanie | Sam/Teljya, Tom, Wendy | Caroline, Max |
| **Core question** | "Is this more powerful than what I already use?" | "Can I understand this without panic?" | "Will this give me ammunition?" | "Will this reduce my load or add to it?" |
| **Key pain** | Provider discovery, jargon | Plan comprehension, paperwork anxiety | Evidence dismissed, zero guidance | Compounding burden, equipment delays |
| **Tech comfort** | High (ChatGPT, iCloud) | Low-moderate (needs visuals, audio) | Wide range (Claude to physical folders) | Wide range (AI professional to email-only) |
| **Pricing** | Plan-funded (AT) | Plan-funded via SC | Plan-funded (AT) | Plan-funded essential |
| **Resonates** | Power, prediction, evidence | Plain English, warmth, simplicity | Empowerment, evidence tools | Burden reduction, practical organisation |
| **Alienates** | Oversimplification, patronising tone | Jargon, complexity | System-apologism, naive optimism | Complexity, assumption of stability |
| **Confidence** | Medium (1 person, 3 sessions) | Medium (2 people, 3 sessions) | High (4 people, 5 sessions) | Medium (2 people, 3 sessions) |

## Divergence Signals

- P-01 vs P-02 diverge → concept works for power users but fails accessibility
- P-03 vs P-04 diverge → concept serves fighters but not maintainers
- ALL participant archetypes approve but SC archetypes don't → too participant-focused for B2B

## Limitations

1. 5 of 8 interview sets are carer-led — participant's own voice often absent or mediated
2. No self-voicing intellectual disability, AAC users, CALD, or Indigenous participants
3. 8 individuals cannot represent 600K+ NDIS participants — grounded hypotheses, not population truths
4. Selection bias: recruited through SC networks — likely more engaged than average
