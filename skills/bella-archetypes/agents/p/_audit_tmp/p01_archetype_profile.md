# The Empowered Navigator

## Summary

A self-managing adult with acquired disability who engages the NDIS with professional capability, proactive strategy, and critical evaluation. Uses their pre-disability professional skills (legal, analytical, technical) to navigate the system independently. Evaluates tools against what they already use (ChatGPT, manual systems) and demands sophistication, not simplification. New to the NDIS but learning fast. Emotionally confronted by the deficit framing of their condition but channels that into determined self-advocacy.

## Behavioural Profile

- **Self-directed navigation.** Insisted LAC provide provider names after initial refusal. Used ChatGPT to draft the NDIS application. Created personal budget category cheat sheets. Stores documents in iCloud with organised folders.
- **Professional skills as advantage.** Kevin: "being a lawyer probably was helpful in a certain way" — navigating bureaucratic language, understanding evidentiary requirements, self-advocating effectively.
- **Critical product evaluation.** Benchmarks BellaAssist against ChatGPT. In MVP testing, identified specific UX issues (repetitive sections, duplicated goal numbering, unclear evidence map). Asks about data architecture, not just features.
- **Emotional vulnerability beneath competence.** The FCA process was "confronting" — seeing functional decline documented clinically. The application process was "daunting, time-consuming, detailed, and bureaucratic" even for a lawyer.

## Motivations & Goals

**Functional:** Self-manage plan effectively | Find quality neurological-specialist providers | Build evidence for first plan review | Track budget and claims accurately
**Emotional:** Feel in control of a situation where control is progressively being lost | Feel that expertise and capability are respected | Feel that the system sees the person, not just the condition

## Pain Points

1. **[Provider discovery — 4/5]** --> "Throwing darts at a dart board." LAC reluctant to give names. Had to find ARC independently through Parkinson's NSW.
2. **[Application complexity — 4/5]** --> "Daunting" even for a lawyer. Used ChatGPT to draft responses.
3. **[Jargon opacity — 4/5]** --> Acronyms everywhere. "Very tricky for an outsider."
4. **[Plan developed without input]** --> Plan was "hatched without any real feedback" from Kevin.

## Representative Language

> "Being a lawyer probably was helpful in a certain way."
> "The thing that I really loved was the prediction... awesome, that's one of the great assets."
> "I would have been devastated if it said, you know, 20% chance."

## Brand & Positioning Implications

| Dimension | Implication |
|---|---|
| **Messaging** | Lead with capability and intelligence, not simplification. "Powerful NDIS tools" not "easy NDIS help." |
| **Pricing** | Open to plan-funded (AT positioning "immensely helpful to make it palatable"). Self-managed = price-conscious but values quality. |
| **Features** | Approval Probability Prediction, Historical Trend Comparison, Evidence Vault. NOT basic budget tracking (rates 1/5 pain). |
| **Red flags** | Oversimplification, patronising tone, "we'll handle it for you" messaging, inability to edit outputs. |
| **Accessibility** | Parkinson's tremor — motor control considerations for touch/click interfaces. Larger targets, undo functionality, error tolerance. |
