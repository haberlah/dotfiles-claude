# The Anchoring Carer

## Summary

A family member managing an ongoing NDIS situation focused on maintaining today's care, not fighting for systemic change. May be in crisis (Caroline: compounding personal stressors, partner with degenerative condition, son with disputed diagnosis) or in stable equilibrium (Max: daughter's plan rolled over 4 years, minimal friction). Either way, the orientation is present-focused: keep things running, don't let anything fall through the cracks, don't add complexity. Evaluates tools through a "net burden" lens — will this reduce my load or add to it?

## Behavioural Profile

- **Present-focused management.** Caroline manages Bob's daily care logistics (SIL transport, provider coordination, equipment gaps) and Oscar's plan without any support. Max maintains Astrid's stable arrangement (same OT provider for entire NDIS journey) with minimal intervention.
- **Delegation where possible.** Caroline chose plan management specifically to reduce admin burden. Max delegates portal authentication to his wife Caroline (different Caroline). Both rely on others for system-facing tasks.
- **Low system confrontation.** Neither is fighting the NDIS. Caroline is too overwhelmed by compounding crises to escalate. Max has no reason to — his plan keeps rolling over. Both would rather maintain what works than rock the boat.
- **Wide capability gap.** Max is an AI professional who asked about data sovereignty and LLM training policies. Caroline struggles to locate documents and stores things as "emails on their phone." Same archetype, radically different tech comfort.
- **Hidden actors.** Both have a partner who handles key functions but is not present in interviews. Max's wife manages portal access. Caroline's ex-partner's interference complicates Oscar's care. These absent voices represent data gaps.

## Motivations & Goals

**Functional:** Keep current support arrangements running smoothly | Track budgets without overspending quarterly allocations | Find appropriate providers when current ones fail | Reduce the paperwork burden of maintaining multiple plans
**Emotional:** Feel that the person they care for is safe and adequately supported | Feel less alone in managing everything | Feel that the system doesn't punish stable situations (Max: latent anxiety about losing the rollover streak) | Feel that crisis situations are acknowledged, not dismissed

## Pain Points

1. **[Compounding personal stressors amplify everything — Caroline]** --> DV, homelessness, family separation, financial hardship — each NDIS frustration lands on top of existing crisis. "It's gone off the rails."
2. **[Equipment/AT delays — Caroline]** --> 3.5 years for a wheelie walker that Bob can't use because he lacks hand strength for brakes.
3. **[Provider quality — Caroline]** --> SIL: "sat in front of a TV for 12 hours." Support workers: "sit there and watch TV."
4. **[Portal login friction — Max]** --> Requires wife's phone every time. Minor but persistent.
5. **[Latent review anxiety — Max]** --> Strongest Kano "if absent" reactions were for review-preparation features. Four years without a review means the eventual one will be high-stakes.
6. **[Scattered documentation — both]** --> Caroline: documents "likely scattered as emails on their phone." Max: organised but relies on wife for portal access.

## Brand & Positioning Implications

| Dimension | Implication |
|---|---|
| **Messaging** | Lead with burden reduction. "Less paperwork, more care." Acknowledge the emotional toll of caregiving without being saccharine. |
| **Pricing** | Plan-funded is essential for Caroline (no personal budget for tools). Max: plan-funded AT positioning "palatable." |
| **Features** | For Caroline: document storage, provider contact directory (her own suggestion), simplified budget view, handover pack. For Max: document intelligence for eventual review prep, handover pack as insurance. |
| **Red flags** | Complexity in onboarding (Caroline cannot learn a complex system right now), assumption of tech literacy, messaging that assumes the carer is coping well, any implication that they should be doing more. |
| **Accessibility** | Caroline: low-tech, needs guided setup, phone-friendly. Max: full digital capability but wants minimal engagement — set-and-forget tools. |
| **Trust** | Caroline trusts Amanda (SC). Max trusts his own judgment. SC-mediated introduction works for Caroline. Max evaluates independently but needs to see the tool is worth the effort. |
