# The Proactive Parent-Advocate

## Summary

A high-agency parent or family member who drives NDIS engagement for their child or family member with strategic intensity. The system is a battleground — they research, coordinate, challenge, and demand. They build "teams" around their loved one, set provider deadlines, and escalate when the system fails. Technology comfort varies widely (from AI-native to physical folders), but the advocacy drive is constant. They evaluate BellaAssist as weaponry: "Will this give me ammunition for the next plan review?"

## Behavioural Profile

- **Strategic system engagement.** Sam bypassed paediatrician waitlists by going directly to a clinical psychologist. Tom compiled all documentation himself and navigated without an SC for 3+ years. Wendy built "Team Tess" with agendas and minutes, lodged formal complaints against failed SCs.
- **Evidence-building orientation.** Tom maps BellaAssist directly into his advocacy workflow. Teljya: "It's like having an NDIS lawyer in your pocket." Wendy describes plan review as needing defensible evidence packages.
- **Team coordination.** Wendy manages care team meetings. Sam/Teljya coordinate OT, speech, psychology across school and clinic settings. Tom fought through court orders to maintain involvement in his son's care.
- **Systemic awareness.** All three families are articulate about what's broken: evidence not being read (Wendy, Tom), deficit framing as retraumatising (Wendy), zero guidance after approval (Sam), and the system rewarding those with professional advantages (Sam: "we're relatively literate and advantaged").
- **Wide tech range.** Sam uses Claude for invoices and asked about LLM data retention. Tom keeps a physical folder and hasn't used the portal since introduction. Wendy delegates digital management to Jane's team. This is the most tech-diverse cluster.

## Motivations & Goals

**Functional:** Build the strongest possible evidence package for plan reviews | Coordinate multiple providers efficiently | Track progress to demonstrate outcomes (or decline) | Translate between family language and NDIS requirements
**Emotional:** Feel that they're doing everything possible for their child/family member | Feel the system acknowledges the stakes | Feel that their effort and expertise are valued, not ignored | Feel that someone is on their side

## Pain Points

1. **[Evidence submitted but not read — CRITICAL]** --> Wendy: review was "bungled," costing thousands. Tom: fought for years building evidence that was ultimately dismissed when funding was cancelled.
2. **[Zero guidance after approval]** --> Sam: "just a bucket of money." No pathway, no introduction, no follow-up.
3. **[Provider coordination burden]** --> Managing multiple providers across settings, tracking progress, ensuring alignment. Wendy: the "literal life-saving" work of coordinating Tess's care team.
4. **[Deficit framing as retraumatising]** --> Wendy acts as "buffer" to protect Tess from deficit-model reports. Sam/Teljya focus on strengths and progress.
5. **[Budget confusion]** --> Sam/Teljya: nearly ran out of quarterly funds twice. Confusion between 24-month plan and quarterly instalments.
6. **[Legal/custody complications]** --> Tom: court orders for shared custody meant NDIS repeatedly excluded him. Unique but illustrative of the system not accommodating non-standard family structures.

## Brand & Positioning Implications

| Dimension | Implication |
|---|---|
| **Messaging** | Lead with empowerment and evidence strength. "Build your strongest case." Acknowledge the fight — don't pretend the system works smoothly. |
| **Pricing** | Plan-funded (AT positioning) resonates strongly. Sam: "immensely helpful to make it palatable." |
| **Features** | Evidence Vault, Approval Probability, Historical Trend Comparison (track progress/decline), Writing Assist (translate family language to NDIS). Budget tracking with quarterly period awareness. |
| **Red flags** | Naive optimism about the system. "We'll make NDIS easy!" — they know it's not easy and won't trust anyone who pretends otherwise. Also: the name "Evidence Vault" (Teljya found it "a bit arresting" and "criminally" coded). |
| **Tech onboarding** | Must accommodate the full spectrum — Sam can self-serve; Tom needs guided setup; Wendy delegates to her SC. Offer SC-mediated onboarding as a pathway. |
| **Trust** | Earned through demonstrated accuracy on their documents. Tom and Wendy have been burned by the system — trust is scarce. Show results on real data. |
